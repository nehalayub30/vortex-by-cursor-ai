A BIT OF HISTORY

Marianne Nems, a visionary artist born in 1969, has embarked on a remarkable artistic journey that seamlessly weaves together influences from a multitude of artistic and intellectual giants. Residing amidst the vibrant energy of Miami Beach, Florida, Nems stands as a testament to the power of artistic expression rooted in a deep appreciation for the masters who came before.

From the outset of her artistic journey, Nems discovered a wellspring of inspiration in the remarkable works of the French Impressionists — Claude Monet, Édouard Manet, Pierre-Auguste Renoir, Edgar Degas, and Camille Pissarro. These luminaries skillfully interwove texture and color palette, utilizing brushstrokes as pixels, as if imbuing their canvases with vibrant atmospheres. The direction and energy of their brushwork breathed life into their paintings, evoking an ineffable vitality.

With 25 years of experience in interior architecture and design, Nems brings an international perspective to her artistic endeavors. Hailing from a lineage of esteemed designers, she carries a family legacy that spans over a century. Her grand cousin, Michel Harmouch, designed the Museum of Beirut in the 1960s, while her father contributed to designing for Prince Suleiman, who now serves as the King of Saudi Arabia, in the 1980s.

In 1997, Nems embarked on a significant move to the United States, settling in the vibrant cultural melting pot of New York City. It was here that she faced the challenges of being a single parent while tirelessly working to support her two children. These experiences shaped her into a fearless and determined individual, embodying a spirit of taking on challenges and pursuing happiness.
